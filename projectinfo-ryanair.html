Ryanair check-in design


In this case, your objective is to design a new interface for check-in kiosks at Copenhagen Airport. Your groups will observe the check-in process, analyze the needs of users, and consider the airline's business requirements.
My group had Ryanair and elderly people.

Process


Discover:
In the discovery phase, we conducted online research about Ryanair, exploring the services they offer and the travel habits of our target audience.
Following this, we ventured to Copenhagen Airport for a field study to observe the challenges and advantages experienced by older individuals at the check-in kiosks. Some were aware of our observations, while others were not.

Define:
We used our observations and research findings to create empathy maps, user journeys, and a value proposition canvas, aiming to understand and visualize the issues faced by our target audience.

Develop:
In the development phase, we crafted a wireflow to simplify the check-in process based on our research, which indicated that older individuals preferred a straightforward and easy experience.

Deliver:
We created a prototype in Figma, which can be viewed here. Subsequently, we applied Jakob Nielsen's heuristics, which serve as essential benchmarks maintaining design standards. Notably, we emphasized the use of signifiers, employing light and color to guide users' focus on the kiosk.
