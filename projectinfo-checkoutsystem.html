Check-out system design


The task is to design a digital prototype allowing the target audience to book an experience. The primary focus should be on the booking process, but also including additional features that cater to the audience's needs, including upselling options during the booking.

Process


We used the Double Diamond model to carry out this project, so it makes sense to go through the process of its phases.

Discover:
First, we brainstormed to figure out what kind of experience we wanted to sell. In the end, we settled on a trampoline park.
We conducted research to understand our competitors: what they were doing and how they were marketing their offerings.
We also gathered quantitative data on holiday habits among Danish families from sources like dst.dk. To get qualitative insights, we directly asked parents and children about their preferences.

Define:
To define our problem and goals, we applied classic UX tools.
We created two personas based on our research, which gave us a clear picture of our users.
We used "How May We" questions to address specific issues, like "How may we get a customer to visit our website again?"
A sitemap was crafted to provide an overview and structure for our website.
We designed a flowchart to visualize the user's journey through our check-out system from the very first click.

Develop:
We established a style guide to ensure a shared understanding of the project's visual elements within our team.
Wireframes were created to better comprehend the website's structure, serving as a starting point for the design.
We also worked on graphic design, including creating a logo and other graphical elements for the project.

Deliver:
A prototype was developed using Figma, allowing the entire team to collaborate on it simultaneously.
We conducted user testing on the prototype using methods like the "Gangster" test and "think-aloud" test with relevant users to ensure that the design was understandable and intuitive.
